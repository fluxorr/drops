# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Drops is a private, single-user learning companion for a technically curious person who wants a small, worthwhile lesson to read each day. They arrive from a browser notification or revisit their history while choosing what to learn next. The primary task is reading and completing one concise lesson; profile and interest management should stay secondary.

## Product Purpose

Drops turns broad interests and an evolving knowledge map into an intentional sequence of sourced daily lessons. The application chooses what comes next, Gemini teaches it, and completion quietly improves the learner model. Success means recommendations build naturally, sources feel trustworthy, and the product becomes a useful personal record without demanding maintenance.

## Brand Personality

Thoughtful, quiet, and assured. The voice should feel like a perceptive mentor who explains why a lesson belongs here, writes clearly, and never performs intelligence for its own sake. The emotional goal is calm curiosity rather than urgency or achievement pressure.

## Anti-references

Drops must not resemble a SaaS analytics dashboard, Notion workspace, Linear clone, Duolingo streak machine, chatbot transcript, or generic productivity tracker. Avoid gamification, achievement language, dense metric cards, decorative AI gradients, glassmorphism, and visual noise that competes with reading.

## Design Principles

1. Reading is the product. Give lesson text, provenance, and continuity the strongest hierarchy.
2. Explain the recommendation. Every generated lesson earns trust by stating why it follows from the learner's interests and knowledge.
3. Keep intelligence quiet. Automation should reduce profile maintenance without turning the interface into a chatbot.
4. Show an accumulated practice. Dates, completion marks, and history should feel like a personal ledger rather than a performance dashboard.
5. Make secondary actions recede. Settings, filters, and controls remain easy to find without interrupting the daily lesson.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA, including visible keyboard focus, semantic landmarks, reliable screen-reader labels, 44px touch targets where practical, and text contrast of at least 4.5:1. Never encode knowledge or completion state by color alone. Honor reduced-motion and system color-scheme preferences. Lesson prose must remain comfortably readable at browser zoom and on narrow Android screens.
